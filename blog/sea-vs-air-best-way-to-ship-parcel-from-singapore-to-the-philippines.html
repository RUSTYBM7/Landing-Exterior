Sea Freight vs Air Freight: Best Way to Ship from Singapore to Philippines


If you're planning to ship from Singapore to the Philippines, one of the first things to consider is how your goods will be transported there. Whether you're sending products for your business or personal items to loved ones, choosing the correct shipping method makes a big difference in terms of cost, speed, and reliability.

Both sea freight and air freight have their advantages and limitations. Understanding the differences between these options helps you avoid delays, reduce expenses, and make informed decisions that match your timeline and cargo needs. In this article, we'll guide you through everything you need to know when shipping from Singapore to the Philippines—from freight types and cost factors to real-life considerations and tips.

What is Sea Freight?

Sea freight, also known as ocean freight, involves moving cargo in shipping containers on cargo vessels. It is widely used for international trade and is often the go-to option for large, heavy, or non-urgent shipments.

Common Sea Freight Options:

- FCL (Full Container Load): You rent an entire container for your goods. Suitable for large volumes.

- LCL (Less Than Container Load): Your goods share a container with shipments from other senders. Cost-effective for smaller volumes.

Typical Sea Freight Duration to the Philippines:

- Port to Port: 7 to 15 days, depending on the shipping line

- Door to Door: Can take up to 21–30 days, including customs clearance

Sea freight is suitable for:

- Furniture, building materials, and equipment

- E-commerce inventory in bulk

- Household goods for relocation

What is Air Freight?

Air freight refers to the transportation of goods via aircraft. This method is preferred for urgent, lightweight, or high-value items that require rapid delivery to the destination.

Air Freight Types:

- Standard air freight: Delivered in the belly of a passenger or cargo aircraft

- Express courier services: Fastest, often includes door-to-door delivery

Typical Air Freight Duration to the Philippines:

- Airport to Airport: 1 to 3 days

- Door to Door (including customs): 3 to 7 working days

Air freight is ideal for:

- Medical supplies and pharmaceutical goods

- Fashion or tech products with short launch cycles

- Documents and sensitive parcels

Cost Factors to Consider

Choosing between sea and air freight also depends on cost structure and budgeting. Here are some factors that affect pricing:

Sea Freight Costs May Include:

- Container rental or LCL fees

- Port charges and handling

- Documentation and customs clearance

- Delivery to the final destination

Air Freight Costs May Include:

- Airway bill and freight charges

- Fuel surcharges

- Customs clearance and taxes

- Last-mile delivery (if needed)

Tip: Use a freight forwarder or courier service, such as Airpak Express, to obtain accurate estimates and avoid hidden charges.

When Should You Choose Sea Freight?

Sea freight is best suited for shipments that are not time-sensitive and involve larger cargo volumes. It also offers better value for money over long distances.

Use sea freight if:

- You're moving bulky goods, such as furniture or machinery.

- Your business restocks inventory on a monthly or quarterly basis.'

- Your client or receiver doesn't require urgent delivery.

- You want to lower freight costs for heavier goods.

When Should You Choose Air Freight?

Air freight is ideal when speed is a priority. If your goods are lightweight but valuable or require urgent delivery, air freight ensures quick and safe transportation.

Use air freight if:

- You're shipping electronics, mobile phones, or laptops.

- You need to send perishable or sensitive goods.

- You're running an e-commerce store with fast delivery needs.

- You have last-minute business or personal deliveries to make.

Considerations for Cross-Border Shipping (Singapore to Philippines)

When shipping from Singapore to the Philippines, it is essential to follow customs regulations to ensure a smooth delivery. Here's what to keep in mind:

Customs and Documentation

- Proforma invoice and packing list

- Airway bill (for air freight) or Bill of Lading (for sea freight)

- Import permits (if required for regulated goods)

- Identification or business documents (for clearance)

Taxes and Duties

The Philippines imposes import duties on goods, depending on their type and declared value. It's essential to classify goods accurately and provide proper documentation to avoid penalties.

Packaging Standards

Both air and sea shipments must be securely packaged. Air freight typically requires lighter but protective packaging, while sea freight must be water-resistant and stackable.

Ports and Airports

- Main Ports in the Philippines: Port of Manila, Port of Cebu, Port of Davao

- Main Airport: Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA)

Real-World Examples of Freight Choice

Business Case A clothing brand in Singapore ships 200 boxes of garments quarterly to the Philippines. Since the goods are not urgent and cost is a factor, they choose sea freight with LCL service.

Personal Case A Filipino worker in Singapore needs to send medical equipment urgently to a family member in Manila. They opt for air freight to ensure delivery in 2 days.

How to Decide: Key Questions to Ask Yourself

If you're still unsure whether to go with sea or air freight, asking yourself these simple questions can help narrow down the right choice for your shipment:


How urgent is the delivery? If time is tight and delivery is required within a few days, air freight is the preferred option. If there's flexibility, sea freight is more economical.


How much are you shipping? Large and heavy items are best sent by sea. Smaller, lighter packages may be more cost-effective by air, depending on the volume of goods being shipped.


What is your budget? Air freight is quicker but pricier. Sea freight costs less but takes longer to arrive. Always compare quotes for both options.


Is your cargo fragile or perishable? Air freight typically involves smoother handling and shorter transit times, making it safer for sensitive or perishable goods.


Do you need door-to-door service? Some logistics providers (like Airpak Express) offer both air and sea options with full end-to-end delivery for your convenience.


Taking a moment to reflect on these questions can save you both time and money, ensuring a smoother shipping experience from Singapore to the Philippines.
