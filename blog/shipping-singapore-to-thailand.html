Duties & Taxes in Thailand Duty

When shipping from Singapore to Thailand, it’s important to be aware of these charges, as they can vary depending on the shipment’s value and destination. We recommend checking these details, including transit times and costs of shipping, before sending your parcels to avoid any surprises.

Thailand Duty & Tax Information

  • Currency: Thai Baht (THB)

  • Calculation Method: CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight)

  • Tax Threshold: 1,500 THB, which may be relevant when calculating the total cost of shipping.

  • Tax (VAT/GST): 7%

  • Duty Threshold: 1,500 THB

  • Commercial Invoice

  • Air Waybills

Some shipments may require customs clearance. If this happens, the sender must provide all necessary paperwork to the Customs Department or other relevant authorities, following the specific regulations of the destination country.

Thailand Shipping Restrictions & Prohibited Items

Shipping parcels to another country can be complex due to customs regulations. Before sending your parcel to Thailand, it’s important to check if special permissions are required or if the item is allowed to enter the country.

Prohibited items can change frequently, so we advise senders to regularly review the latest restrictions to ensure compliance during the shipping process. For the most accurate information, please refer to the official customs websites in both Thailand and Singapore.

Prohibited Items for Shipping to Thailand:

While this list is not exhaustive, here are some items often restricted or prohibited when shipping to Thailand:


  • Perishables: Food and perishable goods.

  • Hazardous Materials: Flammable items (aerosols, certain liquids), dangerous/hazardous goods, toxic powders.

  • Sensitive Items: Indecent/obscene content, religious objects (if deemed offensive/illegal).

  • Documentation: Personal identification documents (passports, driving licenses, credit cards).

  • Electronics & Components: Standalone batteries (or improperly packaged batteries), used/repaired electronic goods.

  • Chemicals & Personal Care: Medication, supplements, cosmetics, and skincare products (often require permits).

  • Valuables: Currency (paper or coin).

  • Illegal & Regulated: Tobacco, e-cigarettes, illegal drugs, or any items related to unlawful activities.

  • Packaging: Improperly packaged goods, luggage containing prohibited items.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) Section

1. Is it easy to ship from Singapore to Thailand?

Yes. Shipping from Singapore to Thailand is straightforward with a reliable courier like Airpak Express. We provide door-to-door shipping, clear customs support, and tracking to ensure your parcel arrives smoothly 

2. How long does shipping from Singapore to Thailand usually take?

Delivery times for shipments from Singapore to Thailand typically range from 3 to 7 business days, depending on the shipping service and whether you choose air freight or other freight options.

3. What is the cheapest way to ship items from Singapore to Thailand?

The cheapest way to ship often depends on your parcel size, weight, and delivery speed. Economy air freight or consolidated cargo warehouse services are good options for cost savings.

4. How do I ship items internationally from Singapore to Thailand?

You can ship items via courier or freight forwarders who specialise in international shipping. Make sure to prepare all required documents and comply with customs regulations for smooth processing.

5. How should I pack my shipment for shipping from Singapore to Thailand?

Pack your items securely using sturdy materials and cushioning to prevent damage during transit. Proper labelling and documentation are essential to meet international shipping standards.

6. How do I track my shipment and know if it’s delayed in customs?

Most couriers provide tracking services where you can check your parcel’s status. If your shipment is delayed in customs clearance, you will usually be notified by your courier or freight forwarder.

7. Do I need insurance when I ship items from Singapore to Thailand?

While not mandatory, insurance is highly recommended to protect your shipment against loss or damage during international freight.

8. Are there any restrictions on items I can ship from Singapore to Thailand?

Yes, certain items are restricted or prohibited from import into Thailand. Always check the latest customs guidelines, including those related to Thailand customs, before sending your parcels to avoid issues.

9. What should I do if my shipment is lost or damaged during transit?

Contact your courier or freight forwarder immediately with your shipment details. They will assist in locating your parcel or filing a compensation claim.
